ABOUT ME

Welcome to my corner of the web! :)

I'm a scientist with a strong foundation in chemistry and material science, driven by a passion for sustainability. My work focuses on developing biomaterials and characterizing biocomposites using advanced characterization techniques. My reseach interests include Nanocellulose, Bio-based materials, Polymers and Composite Materials, Advanced Material Characterization. I'm passionate about transforming waste biomass into high-value products that support a circular economy. My expertise extends to organic synthesis, biochemical analysis, and bioproduct extraction, all of which I've applied in leading research and development projects with multidisciplinary teams.

I completed my PhD in chemistry at the University of Waikato in New Zealand, following my Master's by thesis at Massey University, New Zealand. My journey into the field of science began with a chemistry honors degree from the College of Chemical Sciences (ICHEM) in Sri Lanka. Before continuing postgraduate research, I gained valuable industry experience as a Pharmaceutical Quality Control Chemist.

Music is another one of my passions, helping me find balance and peace in my busy life. When I'm not in the lab, you'll likely find me hiking, traveling, reading, or capturing the beauty of mountains and beaches.

PROJECTS

These are the projects I've been involved in throughout my research journey.

BIOCOMPOSITE DEVELOPMEMT

Developed eco-friendly composite materials by incorporating algae cellulose to enhance the mechanical and thermal properties of thermoplastic polymers for sustainable packaging applications.

Learn more

NANOCELLULOSE PRODUCTION

Investigated the production and characterization of algae-derived TEMPO-oxidized nanocellulose, demonstrating high crystallinity and thermal stability for use as reinforcement fillers.

Learn more

Algae Cellulose Extraction and Characterization

Extracted and characterized cellulose from New Zealand native algae species, assessing their high crystallinity, thermal stability, and potential for use in sustainable composite materials.

Learn more

Cellulose and Biostimulant from Oedogonium grown in wastewater

Developed a two-step biorefinery process using Oedogonium Calcareum biomass from wastewater treatment to extract biostimulants and high-purity cellulose.
